Bylaws of the Alumni and Volunteer Corporation for the Virginia Omicron Chapter of Sigma Phi Epsilon

  1. These Bylaws are to serve as a guideline for membership and activities of the Alumni and Volunteer Corporation for the Virginia Omicron Chapter of the Sigma Phi Epsilon Fraternity. They should act in accordance with the Constitution and Bylaws of the National Headquarters of Sigma Phi Epsilon and content not mentioned or found in conflict with those documents they should take precedent.
  2. The "Alumni and Volunteer Corporation" shall here and for the remainder of the document be named the "AVC".
  3. Members of the Association. A member of the Association is defined as:
    1. Any person who has been initiated into the Virginia Omicron Chapter of Sigma Phi Epsilon Fraternity, a not-for-profit fraternity chartered in accordance with the Bylaws and Administrative Policies and Procedures of Sigma Phi Epsilon Fraternity, a Virginia not-for-profit corporation (the "National Fraternity"), and under the regulations of Lynchburg College, and which person is not an undergraduate at Lynchburg College or at any other college or university; or;
    2. Any person who is a member of the National Fraternity as defined in Article V of the Constitution of the National Fraternity and who is admitted to membership in the Association by 75% vote of the AVC during any of its regularly scheduled annual meetings, or;
    3. Any person who by 75% vote of the AVC at any regular meeting of the AVC is elected to honorary membership in the Association.
    Any such person shall continue to be a member of the Association as long as he adheres to all the principles upon which the Association is based and to the Constitution and Bylaws of the National Fraternity.
  4. Meetings of the Association.
    1. A regular meeting of the members of the Association shall be held at least once every year, at a time and place to be specified by the AVC Executive Board. Such meeting shall be designated the "Annual Meeting." Special meetings of the members shall be held if called by the President of the Association or by a majority of the AVC present at any regular meeting thereof. All meetings of the members shall be held at the principal meeting location of the Virginia Omicron Chapter of Sigma Phi Epsilon.
    2. Written notice of each meeting of the members, stating the purpose for which the meeting is called and the time and place of such meeting, shall be mailed not less than twenty (20) nor more than 60 days before the date of such meeting to each member at his address as it shall appear on the books of the Association. Business transacted at any special meeting shall be confined to the purpose stated in the notice of such meeting.
    3. The presence in person of ten (10) members is required for and shall be necessary to constitute a quorum and be sufficient for the transaction of business at any meeting of the members. If a quorum shall not be present at any meeting of the members, the members present may adjourn the meeting from time to time, without notice other than announcement at the meeting, until a quorum shall be present.
  5. Officers.
    1. The officers of the Association shall be a president, a vice president, a treasurer, a secretary, and such other officers as shall be designated from time to time by the AVC
    2. Officers shall be elected once each year by vote of a majority of the AVC held after the Annual Meeting in such year. Each officer shall be elected to serve for a term of one (1) year and thereafter until his successor shall be elected and shall qualify or until his resignation, death or removal. Any officer may be removed with or without cause, at any time by vote of a majority of the AVC present at any regular meeting of the AVC, successor to serve for the remaining portion of such term.
  6. The Duties of each Officer shall be:
    1. President:
      1. Responsible for the organization and planning of the annual meeting of the AVC;
      2. The liaison between the undergraduate chapter and the AVC;
      3. Contact between the National Organization and the AVC;
      4. Sign off on all checks and expenses in cooperation with the Treasurer of the AVC;
      5. Arrange a meeting in any form with the new President of the undergraduate chapter no later than January 31st of the year following his election;
      6. An agenda should be presented ahead of time to the AVC Executive Board for its approval
      7. The purpose of this meeting shall be introduction and advice to help promote good communication between the two groups and share ideas.
    2. Vice President:
      1. The coordination and communication of AVC events that shall be planned throughout the year as deemed attainable and desirable;
      2. Working in coordination with the President to plan the annual meeting and agenda thereof;
      3. Fulfill the responsibilities of the President should he not be able to do so for any reason as listed in Section V, b of these Bylaws.
    3. Secretary:
      1. The coordination of all forms of communication amongst the AVC, between the AVC and undergraduate chapter, and the AVC and the National Fraternity;
      2. Maintaining minutes and documentation of the AVC and its Executive Board;
      3. Maintaining the AVC web-site at www.sigepvaoalumni.org;
      4. Work with the President and Vice President to plan and coordinate the annual meeting and its agenda.
    4. Treasurer:
      1. Maintain an AVC bank account:
      2. Receiving and sharing account information;
      3. Properly keeping a record of all debits and credits to the AVC account;
      4. Sign off on all checks and expenses with the President of the AVC
      5. Plan an annual budget for the AVC to be presented at the annual meeting;
      6. Plan and coordinate fundraising opportunities annually with the undergraduate chapter's respective Executive Board member, the AVC, and the National Organization;
      7. Present an AVC financial for future AVC expenses and activities;
      8. Work with the AVC in particular to raise money for the AVC as well as the undergraduate chapter;
      9. Work with the undergraduate chapter to fund the Annual Balanced Man Scholarship to be given out each fall semester in the amount found necessary by the undergraduate chapter.
  7. Amendments.

    These Bylaws may be amended by vote of two-thirds of the members of the Association present at any Annual Meeting or at any special meeting of the members of the Association; provided, that notice of the proposed amendment shall have been set forth in the notice of the meeting.
