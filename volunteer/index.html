Volunteer Opportunities

The duties of every Sigma Phi Epsilon are to remember his Oath of Obligation; to follow the teachings of the Ritual; to uphold Virtue, Diligence, and Brotherly Love for the betterment of himself and his brothers that our lives may bring everlasting glory to Sigma Phi Epsilon. Fulfill these duties in your daily life, and you will find your fraternal experience to be rich and rewarding for both you and your brothers."

The role of the alumnus and volunteer should be that of selfless service and often requires personal sacrifice. This role is not one of exploitation and self-gratification to the detriment of the undergraduate chapter and its membership, but one of mutual benefit, great reward, and self satisfaction.

The Sigma Phi Epsilon Virginia Omicron Alumni & Volunteer Corporation (AVC) has many exciting volunteer opportunities for brothers and other individuals that want to donate their time and talents. Below are some of the available volunteer opportunities; however, this is not an exhaustive list and other opportunities do exist. Contact the AVC at sigepvaoalumni@gmail.com to help promote its mission of building balanced men. Please include your name, the position or type of volunteering you are interested in, and a brief explanation of how you can help the AVC.

Fundraising Chair

The Fundraising Chair will work with the Treasurer to develop and maintain fundraising campaigns consistent with the goals and purpose of the AVC. This includes, but is not limited to, setting fundraising goals, organizing alumni events designed to raise money, and managing a fundraising committee. This person should have the ability to function independently, have excellent verbal and written skills, and should be creative and willing to run with ideas.

Events Chair

The Events Chair will work with the Vice President to plan Fall and Spring Alumni Weekends along with other AVC events. They will also develop an events calendar for all AVC and undergraduate events in order to keep Alumni updated. This person will be in charge of implementing regional alumni groups in order to increase AVC activity in certain areas of the country. This person should have experience in organizing events and should have good communications skills.

Networking Chair

The Networking Chair will be in charge of developing the AVC's job and internship opportunities network. This person will have the task of creating a valuable resource for our Alumni and undergraduate members in order to assist them with finding jobs and also filling openings with qualified applicants from our membership. This person will be in charge of planning networking events and communicating with Alumni, parents, and other resources to find out about job and internship opportunities. This person will also be working with the Brother Mentor Coordinator to assist members during the Brother Mentor Challenge.

Scholarship Chair

The Scholarship Chair will work directly with the undergraduate scholarship chair, the Balance Man Steward and the undergraduate VP of Recruitment to develop and award the Balanced Man Scholarship. They will be in charge of the schedule for the application process, the marketing plan, and the review committee for the scholarship. This person should be available to go back to LC for the interview process. They should also have well developed communication skills and should feel comfortable answering questions from applicants and their parents.

Records Chair

The Records Chair has the responsibility of maintaining the key information for the AVC. This includes the minutes from AVC meetings, the contact database and the AVC email account. This person should have the ability to work with excel, word, and familiarity with Gmail and Outlook. They will also have to coordinate with Nationals twice per year to maintain accurate database information.
